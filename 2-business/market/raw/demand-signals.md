## Executive Summary

Demand signals for AI corporate training in China are **strongly positive** across all dimensions: search interest, budget allocation, pilot activity, and willingness to pay. The market is transitioning from "AI curiosity" (2024) to "AI implementation" (2025-2026), with enterprise AI training budgets growing 58% YoY and AI-related course demand surging 798.8% YoY. However, **60%+ of enterprise AI training budgets fail to produce expected ROI** — creating a clear market opening for result-oriented, AI-native training platforms like AI Teacher. Pricing at 800-1,800 RMB/account is **positioned at the low-to-mid range** of the market, making it competitive for penetrating the existing 1,700-client base.

### 4.1 Positive WTP Signals

| Signal | Evidence | Tag |
|--------|---------|-----|
| Enterprise AI budget growth | Average 75% increase expected in next year (June 2025 survey) | [Data] — 新浪财经 |

### 4.2 Negative WTP Signals / Headwinds

|--------|---------|-----|

| AI budget ROI failure rate | >60% of enterprise AI marketing budgets did not produce expected returns | [Data] — 2026 industry report |

| SME capability gap | >70% of SMEs face "want to use AI but can't implement" challenge | [Data] — 2026 survey |

|-----------------|--------------------------|-----|

### 4.4 WTP Conclusion

**WTP is moderate-to-strong, with a critical qualifier:** enterprises are willing to pay for AI training solutions that demonstrably connect to business outcomes. The 53.4% preference for "pay-for-outcomes" is a major signal. AI Teacher's project-based model (RMB 30K/project) aligns well with this — it's a bounded investment with measurable deliverables.

The per-account pricing of RMB 800-1,800 is **within the zone of willingness** given:
- Average per-capita training spend is RMB 1,278 [Data]
- Enterprises are already spending in this range on traditional platforms
- AI value proposition justifies a modest premium

**Risk:** The 60%+ ROI failure rate in AI tools means buyers are increasingly skeptical. AI Teacher must lead with demonstrable outcomes (completion rates, skill application metrics) from Day 1.
